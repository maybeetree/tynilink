What is this?

Tyni.link is a service that lets you shorten long urls like "https://docs.google.com/document/d/1su0sOYptfFfMlxnDsXpCfrtwvnPby4Gop_rX4haHSTw/edit" to short, sweet urls like tyni.link?d=MyHome. Unlike most shorteners, which produce urls similar to goo.gl/zf0I5, Tyni.link lets you choose your own, readable, urls, like the one mentioned above. It also provides different functions like tracking, link monetization, QR code generation and more.

Why is it better than others?

Have you ever been in a situation where you have to write down a link on paper (school project citation, notes, etc.)? Than you would understand how unhandy it is to write randomly generated codes like goo.gl/zf0I5: l's look like I's, 0's look like O's, etc. Also the same happens when you try to say the link to someone: random codes are just too easy to forget. With tyni.link YOU make the code, so you'll never have problems like that again.
